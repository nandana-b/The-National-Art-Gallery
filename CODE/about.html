Over the course of its remarkable history, The National Art Gallery has been an active participant in the Middle Eastern art scene for almost forty years. It has had the privilege of observing and being part of the significant changes that have taken place within the regional art community. Originally known as Ornina and established in 1987, the gallery initially operated as an exhibition space situated above a small bookstore in Homs, Syria. It served as a vital meeting point for intellectuals, critics, and artists of that era. In 1995, The National Art Gallery relocated to a modest villa in Jumeirah, Dubai, where it continued to promote Arab Modernism while also fostering critical discussions that had shaped its early years. Notable exhibitions during this formative period featured artists who have since become esteemed Arab masters, such as Fateh Moudarres, Dia Azzawi, Paul Guiragossian, and Ismail Fattah.


Since 2012, The National Art Gallery has actively participated in numerous international and regional art fairs, showcasing its artists' works at renowned events like Art Basel, FIAC, Frieze, Independent Brussels, The Armory Show, Liste, miart, Art Dubai, Artissima, and many others.
